Good to know before you start.

Clear answers about who Nest is for, what it connects, and how to begin.

Yes. Nest is free to use. You can create a workspace through the existing Google, GitHub, or Discord sign-in options.

Nest is built for college students at any university. The calendar, tasks, notes, files, and chat workspace is not limited to Emory students or tied to a university partnership.

Emory students receive access to local course-planning data, including course search and representative section details. The rest of the Nest workspace is available to students at other colleges.

Choose “Start free,” continue with Google, GitHub, or Discord, and complete the short onboarding flow. Your first sign-in creates your Nest workspace.

Nest stores the account and workspace data needed to provide its features. Authentication and file storage use Appwrite, while other user data is stored in Nest's VPS-hosted databases. See the Privacy Policy for the current details and choices.

You can add a Canvas calendar feed and other compatible HTTP, HTTPS, or webcal iCalendar feed URLs during onboarding or later in Settings.
