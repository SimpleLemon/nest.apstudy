# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Nest.APStudy serves college students who need to plan classes, manage deadlines, and keep course work in one place. The landing page speaks to students at any university; Emory students are a specialized audience because Nest includes local Emory course data and section planning.

## Product Purpose

Nest is a free academic workspace that combines calendar, tasks, courses, notes, files, and chat. The landing page should help a student understand the product quickly and feel confident starting an account. Success is measured by qualified visitors choosing the signup path and completing onboarding.

## Positioning

One focused workspace for the week, the semester, and the work behind both.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: start a free account through the existing OAuth sign-in flow.
- Secondary CTA: explore the product interface before committing.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: “Your semester, in one place.”
- Belief ladder: Nest is for students at any college; it brings the core academic workflow together; the product is tangible and usable; Emory students receive deeper course-planning support; starting is free and straightforward.
- Proof on hand: real interface states and confirmed student affiliation with Texas A&M, UCLA, UC Berkeley, the University of Arizona, and Emory University. University names do not imply endorsement.

## Brand Personality

Capable, calm, and focused. Nest should reduce the feeling of academic fragmentation without becoming sterile, institutional, or overly technical.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS templates, repetitive card grids, unsupported social proof, university partnership language, an Emory-only impression, and an all-dark developer-tool aesthetic.

## Design Principles

- Show the product doing real academic work.
- Make the next action obvious without rushing the visitor.
- Welcome every college student while stating the Emory advantage precisely.
- Use motion to explain or orient, never to hide content.
- Prefer a few decisive compositions over repeated marketing components.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Meet WCAG 2.1 AA contrast and interaction requirements. Support keyboard navigation, reduced motion, readable line lengths, visible focus, and layouts that remain usable on narrow mobile screens and at text zoom.
